Are you tired of being bombarded with ads, pop-ups, and other distractions when you browse the web? Do you struggle to read long articles on cluttered websites? If so, then Mercury Reader could be the solution you've been searching for.

## What is Mercury Reader?

Mercury Reader is a free browser extension that simplifies online reading by removing unnecessary elements from web pages, leaving only the core content. With Mercury Reader, you can focus on what matters – the text, images, and videos – without getting distracted by ads, banners, and menus.

## How to install Mercury Reader?

Mercury Reader is available as an extension for most popular browsers, including Chrome, Firefox, and Safari.

To install Mercury Reader, simply follow these steps:

1. Visit the Mercury Reader website [https://mercury.postlight.com/reader/](https://mercury.postlight.com/reader/).
2. Click on the "Install for [your browser]" button.
3. Follow the on-screen instructions to add the extension to your browser.

Once installed, you can easily activate Mercury Reader by clicking the icon in your browser's address bar. 

## Features of Mercury Reader

Mercury Reader offers several features designed to enhance your online reading experience:

### Simple Interface

Mercury Reader offers a clean, minimalist interface that makes reading easier and more enjoyable. The tool removes all clutter from web pages, leaving only the main content. You can customize the font style and size to suit your preferences, making it easier to read long articles.

### Multiple Themes

Mercury Reader comes with several built-in themes, including dark mode and sepia. You can also create your own custom themes using CSS, allowing you to fully personalize your reading experience.

### Read Later Integration

Mercury Reader integrates with popular read later apps like Pocket, Instapaper, and Evernote. With one click, you can save articles you find while browsing the web and read them later on your preferred device.

### Accessible

Mercury Reader is fully accessible, with support for screen readers and keyboard navigation. This makes it easy for people with disabilities to read online content without barriers.
